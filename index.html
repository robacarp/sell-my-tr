History:

I bought this car from the original owner about 6 years ago. After some engine work he did the engine had seized due to a problem with the thrust bearings and he simply stored the car. He kept it outside but covered for 20+ years until he decided to sell it.

The crankshaft was welded into place at the thrust bearing. A replacement engine block was matched to the pistons and the crankshaft was welded and balanced. The head was not disassembled or reworked.

With a seized engine we had it towed to the garage and worked on it over the course of the next 5 years, finances and time permitting. New engine and rubber components all over and its a fun project car and even a nice driver.

Why I am selling:

Several months ago my daily driver ate its engine and spit back out pieces of broken valve seat. I borrowed a few cars until the Triumph brakes were finished and have been driving daily ever since. I need something more practical and I know a collector would love to treat this excellent quality classic with the highest respect and pampering.

I originally bought this car with hopes of restoring the engine and mechanicals and I have succeeded. I love driving and working on the unique engine and design but the time has come for this TR-7 and I to part ways.

What it needs

  • There are a few quirks that need to be worked out in the electrical system. The most prudent of these is a malfunction in the oil pressure warning light circuit, resulting in the light being illuminated constantly. The engine makes great oil pressure verified by the gauge mounted next to the center console.
  • I believe the battery will need to be replaced. Sometimes it has trouble starting after sitting for a few days. A jump-start pack or jumper cables and it starts right up and runs quick.
  • The switch to toggle between the main headlights and the dip beam is broken. I have a replacement that will be included with the car but I don't have the tools to replace it myself.
  • It needs a suspension rework. The shock absorbers are old and need replaced.
  • The connection from the exhaust manifold and the downpipe is leaky and needs a new gasket
  • The door seals are shrunken and need replaced.
